Abstract

Building upon the foundations laid by our previous work, this paper introduces Arena 5.0, the fifth iteration of our framework for robotics social navigation development and benchmarking. Arena 5.0 provides three main contributions: 1) The complete integration of NVIDIA Isaac Gym, enabling photorealistic simulations and more efficient training. It seamlessly incorporates Isaac Gym into the Arena platform, allowing the use of existing modules such as randomized environment generation, evaluation tools, ROS2 support, and the integration of planners, robot models, and APIs within Isaac Gym. 2) A comprehensive benchmark of state-of-the-art social navigation strategies, evaluated on a diverse set of generated and customized worlds and scenarios of varying difficulty levels. These benchmarks provide a detailed assessment of navigation planners using a wide range of social navigation metrics. 3) Extensive scenario generation and task planning modules for improved and customizable generation of social navigation scenarios, such as emergency and rescue situations. The platform’s performance was evaluated by generating the aforementioned benchmark and through a comprehensive user study, demonstrating significant improvements in usability and efficiency compared to previous versions.

Arena Pipeline

Arena is a platform for developing and benchmarking navigation algorithms in human-centric social environments.

Features


  • Automatic Installation Script

Streamline your setup with our automatic installation script for the Arena-ROSNav environment. Get started quickly on Linux or Windows by following the installation guide in our documentation.

  • Multiple Simulators

Choose from four powerful simulators: Isaac Sim, Unity, Gazebo, and Flatland. Each offers unique capabilities, from photorealistic rendering to efficient 2D simulations.

  • Realistic Simulation Environments

Explore prebuilt, realistic simulation environments including offices, hospitals, canteens, warehouses, and more. These environments are designed to mimic real-world scenarios for robust testing.

  • Dynamic Map Generation

Generate dynamic maps and mazes on-the-fly using our advanced map generation tools. Create varied layouts to test navigation algorithms in diverse settings.

  • Variety of Task Modes for robots and pedestrians

  • Diverse Robot Models

Work with a variety of robots, including the Unitree Go1 quadruped, TurtleBot3, Jackal, and more. Each robot is equipped with sensors like laser scanners for realistic navigation.

  • Advanced Planners

Utilize a range of planners, including our custom ROSNavRL (DRL-based), TEB, DWA, MPC, and others. Combine traditional and learning-based approaches for optimal performance.

  • Social Force Models for Pedestrians

Simulate realistic pedestrian behavior with a variety of social force models, including group dynamics and human-robot interactions, powered by frameworks like HuNavSim.

  • Evaluation Pipeline

Benchmark navigation approaches using our Arena Evaluation package. Analyze performance with standard metrics like efficiency, safety, and smoothness.

  • Training Pipeline

Train planner agents using reinforcement learning with Stable Baselines3. Customize training through a user-friendly interface and deploy agents seamlessly.

  • Modular and Flexible Structure

Extend Arena-ROSNav with new functionalities and approaches thanks to its modular structure. Integrate custom planners, environments, or simulators with ease.

  • Move Base Flex Integration

Fully integrated with Move Base Flex, our ecosystem enhances navigation performance and supports customization for diverse robotic applications.
